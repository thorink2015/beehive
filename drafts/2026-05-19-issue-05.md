## The Window

Corn hit 76 percent planted the week ending May 17, six points ahead of the five-year average and dead even with last year. Soybeans reached 67 percent, a full 14 points ahead of the average pace. Tennessee was 96 percent done, North Carolina 94, Illinois 75. Winter wheat slipped again to 27 percent good to excellent, and the earliest wheat is just starting to come off in the Southern Plains.

The catch this week is weather. Multiple storm systems are lined up to drop heavy rain, severe weather, and colder air across much of the country. Read that as a stop-start spray window and a lot of waiting on fields to dry. The smart play is to stop chasing the last planting acres and start booking the fungicide passes that pay the back half of the season. Corn fungicide at tassel and the wheat flag-leaf window are where the real money sits from here on. Get those farmers committed now, before the calendar tightens.

## The Going Rate

The number making the rounds is $8 an acre, and you need to understand it before you match it.

:::stat $8/acre
The low end of the 2026 Iowa State survey, well under the $12.50 average. A volume play on big flat ground, not a price to match on tough fields.
:::

It shows up on big, square, wide-open Corn Belt blocks where a guy with a T50 and a good trailer can run sunup to sundown without ever turning hard. Here's the trap. The $8 floor only works if you've got the scale and the flat ground to fly all day with almost no downtime. If you're running 120-acre fields in hilly, tree-lined, irregular country, quoting $8 is how you go broke politely. Price to your real cost and to the value of hitting the window on time. Let the high-volume crowd fight over the floor. You sell timing and access, not the cheapest number on the block.

## The Fine Print

New label homework, and it lands on the applicator. EPA's Final Herbicide Strategy is now live, and Liberty ULTRA, the BASF glufosinate, is the first row-crop herbicide registered under it. If you fly Liberty ULTRA or either Enlist product this season, you are required to check EPA's Bulletins Live! Two website within six months before the application, for each field, to see whether it sits in a Pesticide Use Limitation Area. Then you bank the required runoff and erosion mitigation points.

Only about 2,000 acres are restricted nationwide right now, so most fields clear with no trouble. But "most" is not "all," and the printed bulletin has to live in your records in case anybody asks.

:::note Before you fly, pull the bulletin
Check EPA's Bulletins Live! Two for each field within six months of application, then print the bulletin and file it. Reg numbers to keep handy:

- **Liberty ULTRA**: 7969-500 (needs three mitigation points)
- **Enlist One**: 62719-695
- **Enlist Duo**: 62719-649
:::

Two minutes of homework beats a FIFRA letter.

## Open Acres

Now turn that homework into money. Most farmers have no interest in figuring out Bulletins Live! Two or counting mitigation points, and the $8 guy sure isn't doing it for them. You already are. So say so out loud. "I pull the EPA bulletin and keep the records as part of the job" is a real reason to hire you over the cheap quote. When the service saves a farmer a fine, price stops being the only thing he's shopping. That's how you win work without racing anybody to the bottom.

## The China Watch

Good news, for once. On Friday, May 8, the FCC's engineering office released Public Notice DA 26-454, extending the waiver that lets already-authorized DJI and Autel drones keep getting software and firmware updates. The old cutoff was January 1, 2027. The new one is at least January 1, 2029, and they widened it to cover bigger updates, not just minor patches.

The reasoning is the interesting part. The agency basically admitted that cutting off security patches on the millions of DJI and Autel drones already flying would create a worse cybersecurity mess than the ban was meant to fix. It even signaled it wants to make the waiver permanent through rulemaking. What this means for you: the bird in your trailer is not on a firmware death clock. It keeps getting security and bug fixes for three more years. The Covered List still blocks new DJI purchases, so this changes nothing about your next bird. But the one you own just got a longer, safer life. Take the win.
